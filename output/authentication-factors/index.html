Authentication factors

Elements used to verify a user's identity.

Description

Authentication factors are critical components in the field of Identity and Access Management (IAM) that help ensure that the entity requesting access to a system or resource is indeed who they claim to be. These factors are divided into three main categories: something you know (knowledge-based), something you have (possession-based), and something you are (biometric-based). Knowledge-based factors include passwords and PINs, which require users to remember specific information. Possession-based factors involve physical objects such as smart cards, security tokens, or mobile devices that generate one-time codes. Biometric-based factors leverage unique physical characteristics like fingerprints, facial recognition, or voiceprints. The implementation of multiple authentication factors, known as multi-factor authentication (MFA), significantly enhances security by adding layers of verification, making it more challenging for unauthorized users to gain access. Organizations often adopt various authentication factors based on their security requirements and the sensitivity of the data being protected.

Examples

  • Password and PIN (knowledge-based)
  • Smart card or mobile token (possession-based)
  • Fingerprint or facial recognition (biometric-based)

Additional Information

  • MFA combines two or more authentication factors for enhanced security.
  • The choice of authentication factors depends on the organization's risk assessment.
